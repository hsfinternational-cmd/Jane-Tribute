The Journey

A Life Well Lived

Before the uniform, there was a young girl from Kyabugeiza, Ibanda with an unbreakable spirit. Born on 6th October 1940, Jane Nancy was raised with the values of hard work and faith that would define her entire life.

On 1st July 1963, less than a year after Uganda gained her independence, Jane was enlisted as a Cadet — an Assistant Superintendent of Police (Woman). She did not arrive in a country ready to receive her; she helped build the country that would.

Throughout her years of service, she lectured at the Police Training School, supervised transport and the Fire Brigade, oversaw guards at parastatals and government buildings, and was responsible for the conduct of more than five thousand officers and civilian staff.

In 1973, while heading the Women Police office, Jane petitioned the President of Uganda to amend the Police Standing Orders so that women police officers could carry firearms. The petition was granted. That same year — almost as if to prove her own case — she discovered her talent as a target shooter.

She headed the Uganda team at the All Armed Forces International Shooting in Ghana in 1976, bringing home the gold. Her legacy continues through the many officers she mentored and the doors she opened for women in the security forces.

"I served because I believed our country was worth serving — and our daughters worth defending."
— SSP (RTD) Nakabiri Jane Nancy
